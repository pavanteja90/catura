Proof of Concept

At Catura, we help set up a proof of concept as a Smart Contract to help protect the interests of the concept owner. A proof of concept is the representation or a demonstration of a product, method or idea in principle to demonstrate its feasibility and to verify if the concept or theory has practical potential. While a PoC is not a complete product, it is still considered sensitive, especially if it is proprietary and represents a significant loss of value if stolen or replicated without explicit permissions. This is also the case when the PoC is sent to investors and other stakeholders, as it records every transaction and any changes made to it.

Catura also assists companies that want to implement blockchain technology for themselves and help our clients set their strategy in motion to deploy blockchain solutions. We sit with our clients and ideate what their specific needs are and once a conceptual workflow, and a blockchain model has been established along with the scope of the project, we help them create a functioning proof of concept for pilot testing. Moreover, once it tried and tested for a period of time, we move into the implementation phase where we scale it for enterprise-wide use.
