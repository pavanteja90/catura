Security Token Offering

At catura, we help companies set up security token offering for transactions. A security token is similar to real securities which represent tokenised assets. Security tokens act as a right to a share of an asset, and because there is also a stricter legal framework, these tokens can represent actual equity, acting as “digital shares” of a company. It is not always tied to equity in all cases, and security tokens can be used to fractionalize ownership in a multitude of assets which range from real estate to fine art as they can grant the token holder an array of rights. It can be equity ownership, periodic dividends, debt repayments, cash flows, voting rights and more and all these rights are codified by a smart contract that governs the token.

More importantly, due to the nature of these tokens, we treat them very differently from both a legal and regulatory perspective. As their values are derived from actual securities, security tokens are considered investments and can have additional regulatory or reporting requirements for the issuing entity.

We provide enhanced regulatory oversight that security token issuance generally requires and ensure better investor protection and more rights for investors thus restoring the balance of power from the viewpoint of a prospective stakeholder. The additional regulatory responsibilities may also include tax reporting, compliance duties along with added transparency. Any failure to adhere to the securities laws or should companies fail to honour their reporting obligations could result in severe penalties. Catura takes care of all these requirements to ensure peace of mind for all parties involved.
